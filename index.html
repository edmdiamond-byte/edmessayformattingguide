EDM Essay Formatting Guide

APA (7th edition) • MLA (9th edition) • Essay structure & citation tools

Overall Essay Formatting

(May change depending on instructor's preference.)

  • Double-spaced throughout
  • Times New Roman, 12-point font
  • 1-inch margins
  • 0.5-inch paragraph indent

Essay Outline Guide

  • Introduction: Hook, background, thesis (A thesis statement is the main claim or argument of the essay.)
  • Body: Topic sentence, evidence, explanation.
  • Conclusion: Restate thesis, summarize key points, final insight.
  • Final Pages:
    For APA, the last page is titled References.
    For MLA, the last page is titled Works Cited.
    Listed references must be in alphabetical order and use *hanging indent.
    *Hanging indent: the first line starts at the margin and all remaining lines are indented.

APA In-text Citations

Source with 1 author (Smith, 2021) - Author last name + source publication year.
Source with 2 authors: (Smith & Jones, 2021)
Source with 3 or more authors: (Smith et al., 2021)
Source with no author: ("Article Title," 2021)

MLA In-text Citations

Source with 1 author (Smith 23) - Author name + page number. No comma required.
Source with 2 authors: (Smith and Jones 56)
Source with 3 or more authors: (Smith et al. 21)
Source with no author: ("Article Title" 10)

MLA In-text Citations

Quotes, Paraphrases, and Signal Phrases

Quotes: It is best to include evidence found in your sources that backs up ideas presented in the paper. This can be done by copying and pasting the evidence close to your statements, adding quotes around their words. In-text citations must follow direct quotes.


Paraphrases: To paraphrase means to rewrite, in your own words, statements found within source text. It is an alternative to directly quoting the source text.

  • The key to a good paraphrase is to maintain the author's meaning.
  • Whether paraphrasing or quoting directly, the source must be cited.


Signal phrases: Signal phrases are words used to introduce a quote or paraphrase. See examples below.

APA Signal Phrase Examples (examples represent paraphrases and quotes from sources)

  • The more we write, the better we become at it (Smith, 2020).
  • According to Wilson (2021), "beginning with an outline helps students organize their writing".
  • Withers et al.(2010) argued the importance of revising drafts before submitting.
  • Fountain (2013) states that, "citing sources protects credibility".
  • Another way to improve writing is to read regularly(Garcia et al., 2018).
  • Williams and Oliver (2015) stated that study groups increase learning retention.
  • Researchers (Mercer & Dennis, 2023) note that reading drafts aloud helps students catch writing errors (p.25).

MLA Signal Phrase Examples (examples represent paraphrases and quotes from sources)

  • The more we write, the better we become at it (Smith 59).
  • According to Wilson, "beginning with an outline helps students organize their writing" (15).
  • Withers et al. argued the importance of revising drafts before submitting (44).
  • Fountain states that, "citing sources protects credibility" (10).
  • Another way to improve writing is to read regularly (Garcia et al. 18).
  • Williams and Oliver (21) stated that study groups increase learning retention.
  • Researchers (Mercer and Dennis 19) note that reading drafts aloud helps students catch writing errors (p.25).
